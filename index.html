Day 1
Alfama & Historic Lisbon
Monday, June 22
Tram 28 route Fado dinner
Stops
10:00am
Lisbon Cathedral (Sé de Lisboa)
Oldest church in Lisbon · Built 1147 · Free entry
›
🚃 Tram 28 — stops right outside 🚶 15 min walk from Bica
Lisbon's oldest church, built in 1147 on the site of a mosque. Stunning Romanesque facade. Free to enter the main nave. A must-see starting point for exploring Alfama.
11:00am
Tram 28
Iconic yellow tram through historic streets
›
🚃 Hop on near the Cathedral
Lisbon's most famous tram winds through Alfama's impossibly narrow streets. Buy a single ticket on board (~€3) or use the Viva Viagem card. Can get very crowded — go early or be prepared to wait. Ride it up through Alfama toward Graça.
11:30am
São Jorge Castle
~€15pp · Moorish hilltop castle · Best views in Alfama
›
🚃 Tram 28 to top of Alfama 🚶 5 min walk up
Hilltop Moorish castle with sweeping 360° views over Lisbon and the Tagus river. Allow 1.5 hrs. Pre-book at castelodesaojorge.pt to skip queues. Peacocks wander the grounds.
📎 castelodesaojorge.pt
1:30pm
Alfama Wander & Lunch
Cobblestone streets, local tascas, street art
›
Get lost in the narrow streets of Alfama. Oldest neighbourhood in Lisbon, Moorish roots. Find a local tasca for lunch — grilled chicken, bifanas, or sardines for Gabby. Cheap and delicious.
5:00pm
Miradouro da Graça
Best sunset viewpoint in Lisbon · Local favourite
›
🚶 10 min walk from Alfama
The best miradouro (viewpoint) in Lisbon — less touristy than others, locals come here with drinks to watch the sunset. Views over the city rooftops, the Tagus, and the 25 de Abril bridge. Arrive before sunset for the best spot.
8:00pm
Fado Dinner
Traditional Portuguese music — Alfama is the home of Fado
›
Tasca do Chico or A Baiuca are legendary small Fado houses in Alfama. Book ahead — they fill up fast. Fado is mournful, soulful Portuguese music performed live while you eat. Completely unique experience. Budget €35–50pp including food and wine.

Belém & Monuments
Wednesday, June 24
Pastéis de Belém UNESCO Monastery
Stops
9:30am
Pastéis de Belém
Original pastel de nata bakery · Open since 1837
›
🚃 Tram 15E from Praça da Figueira to Belém
Rua de Belém 84–92. The original and best pastel de nata. Been making them since 1837 with a secret recipe. Order them warm, sprinkle with cinnamon and powdered sugar. Queue moves fast. Go first thing before it gets too busy.
10:30am
Jerónimos Monastery
~€12pp · UNESCO · Most beautiful building in Portugal
›
🚶 2 min walk from Pastéis de Belém
16th century Manueline monastery — the most ornate and beautiful building in Portugal. UNESCO World Heritage Site. Vasco da Gama is buried here. The cloisters are jaw-dropping. Pre-book at mosteirojeronimos.pt. Allow 1.5 hrs.
📎 mosteirojeronimos.gov.pt
12:30pm
Tower of Belém
~€12pp · Iconic riverside tower · Symbol of Lisbon
›
🚶 15 min walk along the river from Monastery
16th century fortified tower on the banks of the Tagus. One of the most photographed spots in Portugal. Pre-book at torrebelem.gov.pt — queues can be long. Climb to the top for river views. Allow 45 min.
2:00pm
Igreja de São Vicente de Fora
Beautiful azulejo tile panels · Rooftop views
›
🚃 Tram back toward Alfama
Monastery church in Alfama with extraordinary blue and white azulejo tile panels depicting scenes from Aesop's fables. Small entry fee. Climb to the rooftop for panoramic views over Alfama and the river.
4:00pm
LX Factory
Cool converted industrial space · Shops, food, bars
›
🚃 Tram 15E back toward central Lisbon
Former industrial complex converted into a creative hub. Independent bookshops, vintage clothing, restaurants, rooftop bar. Very cool atmosphere. Sunday has a big market but worth visiting any day. Great spot for late afternoon drinks.
